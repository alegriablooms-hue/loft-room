Good news: the ceiling now works for a real loft

At 101.5" (8'5½"), a standing loft is viable — IKEA VITVAL (needs 94½") fits, and the Wayfair Harriet Bee and Target loft beds (~65–69" tall, ~54" to the loft base) leave full headroom below for the desk and plenty above. Only IKEA's tall STORÅ (needs 106") still misses, by ~4½".

How the three zones fit

Back/top wall (solid): loft bed up top, office desk + chair underneath, ladder at the left end — the only wall without a window or door.

Left wall (60" window): sewing bench for daylight, pegboard above, drawers below.

Right wall: cube storage + rolling cart in the solid middle, between the 26" closet (top) and the 28" door (bottom).

Watch-outs

• The room has 2 windows (60" left, 50" bottom) and 3 doors (26" closet + 28" on the right wall, 29" entry on the bottom) — the top wall is the only solid run.

• Keep desk-height pieces under the windows so daylight isn't blocked; leave swing clearance for all three doors.

• Confirm the loft footprint (~42" × 80" twin) fits the 101" top wall and clears the top-right closet before buying.
